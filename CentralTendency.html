Average (Arithmetic Mean)

Measures of Central Tendency Series

An average (Mean) is a simple figure which sums up the characteristics of a whole group of figures. An average is more or less a central value around which various values cluster.

Mathematical Definition

The Arithmetic Mean is the sum of all observations divided by the total number of observations.

$$ \text{Mean } (\bar{X}) = \frac{x_1 + x_2 + \dots + x_n}{n} = \frac{\sum x}{n} $$

Computation by Data Type

Series 1

1. Individual Observations (Discrete Data)

Used when individual values are given. Simply add all values and divide by the count ($n$).

Example: Average income of 10 individuals (10, 90, 85, 103, 11, 29, 84, 15, 35, 80).

$$ \bar{X} = \frac{\sum x}{N} = \frac{542}{10} = 54.2 \text{ Rs} $$

Median

Median is defined as the size of the $(\frac{N+1}{2})^{th}$ item when the items are arranged in the ascending order of magnitude.

Case 1

1. Individual Observations

For ungrouped data, arrange the values in ascending order. The Median is the value of the middle item.

$$ \text{Median} = \text{Size of } \left( \frac{N+1}{2} \right)^{th} \text{ item} $$
Case 2

2. Discrete Frequency Distribution

In the discrete case, the Median is defined as the size of the $(\frac{N+1}{2})^{th}$ item, where $N = \sum f$. We use cumulative frequency to locate this item.

$$ \text{Median} = \text{Size of } \left( \frac{\sum f + 1}{2} \right)^{th} \text{ item} $$
Case 3

3. Continuous Series

For grouped data, we first find the median class using $N/2$. Then, we apply the interpolation formula:

$$ \text{Median} = L_1 + \frac{\frac{N}{2} - cf}{f} \times c $$

Where:

  • $L_1$: Lower limit of the median class.
  • $N$: Total frequency ($\sum f$).
  • $cf$: Cumulative frequency of the class just preceding the median class.
  • $f$: Frequency of the median class.
  • $c$: Class interval width.
